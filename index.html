Overall

Bring Me the Horizon (BMTH) is a British rock band formed in Sheffield, England, in 2004. Originally rooted in deathcore, their early work was marked by intense screaming vocals, heavy breakdowns, and dark themes. Over time, however, BMTH has undergone a dramatic stylistic evolution, incorporating elements from metalcore, alternative rock, electronic, pop, and even hip-hop.

The band is fronted by Oliver Sykes, whose raw vocal style and emotional lyrics have played a major role in defining their sound and persona. Other core members include Lee Malia (lead guitar), Matt Kean (bass), Matt Nicholls (drums), and Jordan Fish (keyboard and programming), whose addition in 2012 was pivotal in expanding the band’s musical direction.

BMTH broke into the mainstream with albums like Sempiternal (2013), which balanced aggression with melody and marked a turning point in their sound. Subsequent releases like That’s the Spirit (2015) and amo (2019) showcased their willingness to experiment, blending genres and pushing the boundaries of modern rock. Their more recent works, including the Post Human series, further explore themes like societal collapse, mental health, and resilience.

Known for their high-energy live shows, introspective lyrics, and ever-evolving sound, Bring Me the Horizon has become one of the most influential and genre-defying bands of their generation
